Causa Rellena Recipe

Description

Causa rellena is one of the most representative dishes of Lima. In addition to its light and delicious flavor, it has a history behind it. This dish is so popular that it is often sold at various food fairs and is a must-have on the family table.

Ingredients

  • 1 kilogram of yellow potatoes
  • 1 chicken breast
  • 1 cup of shelled corn
  • 1 cup of chopped green beans
  • 1 cup of chopped carrots
  • 5 sprigs of cilantro
  • 2 avocados
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs
  • 3 yellow chili peppers
  • 2 lemons
  • 3 tablespoons of oil
  • Mayonnaise

Steps

  1. First, wash the yellow potatoes thoroughly and place them in a pot. Add water until the potatoes are covered and bring to a boil for half an hour. After that time, poke them with a fork; if it goes in easily, they are ready. Drain the water and let the potatoes cool slightly.
  2. Second, while the potatoes are still boiling, start chopping the vegetables. Cut the carrots into small cubes and the green beans into small cylinders. Then prepare a medium pot and add the carrots, green beans, and shelled corn. Cover with water and bring to a boil over medium heat. Cook for about 10 minutes, then let them cool.
  3. Third, while the potatoes are still boiling, cook the chicken breast. Place it in a medium pot and cover with water. Let it boil for at least 30 minutes, then check with a fork to see if it is cooked. Once ready, remove the chicken from the water and let it cool.
  4. Fourth, after turning off the heat for the potatoes and letting them cool, peel and clean the yellow chili peppers. Remove the veins and seeds. Blend what remains and set it aside to add later to the mixture.
  5. Fifth, when the potatoes have cooled enough to handle but are still warm, begin peeling them. As you peel, mash the cooked potatoes with a fork or potato masher and place them in a large bowl.
  6. Sixth, once all the potatoes are mashed, start seasoning. First add the yellow chili paste, then the oil, followed by salt and pepper to taste, and finally the lemon juice. Mix everything with your hands, tasting the salt from time to time. Once ready, set aside for assembly.
  7. Seventh, shred the chicken breast for the filling. Divide the chicken into four parts. Take one quarter and mix it with the cooked vegetables. Add a little mayonnaise and set aside. To the remaining chicken, add mayonnaise and also set aside.
  8. Eighth, divide the potato mixture into three parts. Prepare a springform cake pan. Grease the pan with oil and add the first layer of potato at the bottom, pressing it evenly with your hands. Add the shredded chicken and spread it evenly with a spoon. Then add another layer of potato and flatten it to avoid gaps. After that, add the vegetables and cover with the final layer of potato.
  9. Ninth, let the mixture settle well. Prepare a plate or base for unmolding. If using a springform pan, simply remove the ring. If using a regular pan, carefully flip it onto the serving base and gently tap the bottom so the mixture releases.
  10. Finally, once unmolded, decorate with sliced avocado and sliced hard-boiled eggs. You can also add some mayonnaise and chopped cilantro leaves.
